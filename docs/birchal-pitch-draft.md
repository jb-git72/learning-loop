# Birchal Pitch Script — Draft v1

## Context

Birchal is Australia's leading equity crowdfunding platform, now raising for itself. Key narrative elements:
- Previous raises: $3M (2022, $1/share), $2.4M (2023, 50c), $2M (2024, 50c)
- This round: ~8c/share — massive reduction, but matched by institutional investors ($1M SAFE note)
- Strategic reset: new leadership, new direction (post-transaction services, compliance, registry)
- 330 campaigns, 8 years, largest investor database in Australian CFE
- New founder network outside previous leadership's circles
- PR angle: "Birchal taking its own medicine on valuations"

## The Script

### Version A: "Taking Our Own Medicine" (~90 words, ~38 seconds)

> Hi, I'm [Name], and I lead Birchal — Australia's equity crowdfunding platform. Over eight years, we've helped 330 companies raise capital from everyday investors. But we're going to be honest with you. The market's been tough, and our share price reflects that — down to around eight cents. Here's what's changed: for the first time, institutional investors have come in alongside us. A million dollars on a SAFE note. And we're offering our existing investors — and new ones — the exact same terms. Same price as the professionals. If you believe the best time to back a platform is when it's resetting, not when it's peaking — come take a look.

### Beat-by-Beat Mapping

| Beat | Template slot | Birchal version | Emotion |
|------|--------------|-----------------|---------|
| 1. Identity | FOUNDER NAME + ROLE | "I'm [Name], and I lead Birchal" | Trust |
| 2. Credibility | WHAT THE COMPANY DOES | "Australia's equity crowdfunding platform" | Authority |
| 3. Scale proof | PROOF 1 | "330 companies… eight years" | Confidence |
| 4. The wound | THE WOUND | "market's been tough, share price reflects that — eight cents" | Honesty / vulnerability |
| 5. Pivot proof | THE PIVOT | "institutional investors have come in… million dollars SAFE note" | Validation shift |
| 6. Parity proof | PROOF 2 | "same terms… same price as the professionals" | Fairness / belonging |
| 7. Identity ask | THE ASK | "best time to back a platform is when it's resetting" | Contrarian conviction |
| 8. CTA | LOW-FRICTION CTA | "come take a look" | Curiosity, not pressure |

### Emotional Arc

**Trust → Confidence → Honest vulnerability → External validation → Fairness → Contrarian conviction**

This inverts the Our Cow formula. Instead of wound→pivot→proof, it's proof→wound→validation. The credibility comes FIRST because the wound (price drop) needs a trust bank to land without scaring people off.

### Version B: "Platform That Practises What It Preaches" (~85 words, ~35 seconds)

> Hi, I'm [Name] from Birchal. We've spent eight years helping three hundred and thirty companies raise money from everyday Australians. Now it's our turn. We've just brought in our first institutional investors — a million dollars — and we're offering you the same deal, at the same price. Eight cents a share. Yes, that's lower than before. We think that's a feature, not a bug. We've always told founders: price your round honestly, and the right investors will come. We're taking our own advice. Come see what's changed.

### Why This Version Works

- **"Now it's our turn"** — flips the script. They've been the platform; now they're the company. Meta-narrative.
- **"We think that's a feature, not a bug"** — reframes the price drop as integrity, not failure
- **"We're taking our own advice"** — the single strongest line. It's the proof and the pitch in one sentence.
- **"Come see what's changed"** — CTA that promises a story, not a transaction

---

### Version C: "The Professionals Are In" (~80 words, ~33 seconds)

> I'm [Name], and I run Birchal — the platform that's helped three hundred and thirty Australian companies raise from everyday investors over eight years. For the first time in our history, institutional money is backing us. A million dollars. And we're giving our community the exact same terms — eight cents a share. That's a reset from previous rounds, and we're not hiding from it. We've got new leadership, a new strategy, and for the first time, the professionals at the table. Come find out why.

### Why This Version Works

- Leads with the NEW information (institutional backing) as the hook
- "We're not hiding from it" — Confession hook. Radical transparency builds trust.
- "The professionals at the table" — social proof from a new direction (smart money validates)
- "Come find out why" — Curiosity Gap CTA

## Key Objections to Preempt (pick 1–2 per version)

| Objection | Preemption in script |
|-----------|---------------------|
| "Price dropped, must be failing" | "Market's been tough… institutional investors came in" — external validation |
| "Why would I invest at 8c when others paid $1?" | "Same terms as the professionals" — parity framing |
| "Previous leadership left?" | "New leadership, new strategy" — reset, not retreat |
| "CFE market is dead" | 330 campaigns, 8 years — survival IS the proof |
| "Am I throwing good money after bad?" | "Best time to back is when it's resetting, not peaking" — contrarian frame |

## PR Angles (for social/earned media, not the pitch itself)

1. **"Birchal takes its own medicine on valuations"** — the platform that tells founders to price honestly just did it themselves
2. **"The case for investing in a down market"** — evergreen contrarian investing narrative
3. **"First institutional money in Australian CFE history?"** — the $1M SAFE note is genuinely newsworthy
4. **"New founder brings fresh network"** — leadership transition as renewal, not decline

## Notes

- All versions stay CFE meta-ad compliant: no guaranteed returns, no risk minimisation
- The 8c price is the elephant in the room — every version addresses it head-on rather than hiding it
- Recommended: film all three, A/B test as pre-roll before the campaign page
- The "taking our own advice" line in Version B is the standout — consider building the whole campaign around that concept
