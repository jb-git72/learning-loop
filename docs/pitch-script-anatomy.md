# Pitch Script Anatomy: The Our Cow Blueprint

Analysis of a high-performing CFE pitch video script, scored against our rubric system.

## The Script (33 seconds, 80 words)

> "Hi everyone, I'm Dave, co-founder of Our Cow, where we deliver grass-fed, free-range, and organic meat across Australia. After facing severe drought and bushfires in 2019, we pivoted to selling our own meat online, and now we work with over a hundred farmers. Our business has tripled in the last six months, and we currently have over 7,000 subscription members who receive a box of meat each month. I'm excited to invite you to become a business partner and own shares in Our Cow. If you're interested, please jump onto our website to learn more!"

## Abstract Structure

```
FOUNDER IDENTITY (2s) → CREDIBILITY ANCHOR (4s) → ORIGIN WOUND (5s)
→ PIVOT PROOF (4s) → SCALE PROOF × 3 (10s) → IDENTITY-FRAMED ASK (4s)
→ LOW-FRICTION CTA (3s)
```

### Beat-by-Beat Breakdown

| Beat | Tactic | What it does | Time |
|------|--------|-------------|------|
| 1. Identity | Founder Story | "I'm Dave, co-founder" — founder as guide (StoryBrand) | 0–3s |
| 2. Credibility anchor | — | "grass-fed, free-range, organic… across Australia" — passes Receptionist Test | 3–7s |
| 3. Origin wound | Founder Story | "severe drought and bushfires in 2019" — the villain | 7–12s |
| 4. Pivot proof | BAB (Before→After→Bridge) | "we pivoted to selling our own meat online" — bridge | 12–16s |
| 5. Scale proof | Social Proof Stack | "over a hundred farmers" — proof layer 1 (supply) | 16–19s |
| 6. Momentum proof | Social Proof Stack | "tripled in the last six months" — proof layer 2 (growth) | 19–22s |
| 7. Community proof | Social Proof Stack | "7,000 subscription members" — proof layer 3 (demand) | 22–26s |
| 8. The ask | Direct Offer | "become a business partner and own shares" | 26–30s |
| 9. CTA | — | "jump onto our website to learn more" — low-friction | 30–33s |

**Combined tactics:** Founder Story + Social Proof Stack — the two highest-trust tactics in our library.

## Emotional Architecture

| Beat | Emotion | Mechanism |
|------|---------|-----------|
| "I'm Dave, co-founder" | Trust | Founder vulnerability — Confession hook variant |
| "grass-fed, free-range, organic" | Values alignment | Identity signalling — "I'm one of you" |
| "severe drought and bushfires" | Empathy / pain | Shared national trauma (every Australian felt 2019) |
| "we pivoted" | Resilience / admiration | Underdog narrative — they didn't quit |
| "over a hundred farmers" | Collective purpose | It's not just Dave — it's a movement |
| "tripled… 7,000 members" | Confidence / momentum | Social proof creates FOMO without saying "limited" |
| "become a business partner" | Belonging / pride | Not "invest" — "partner." Identity upgrade |
| "own shares in Our Cow" | Ownership / agency | You're not buying a product, you're joining a story |

**Emotional arc:** Trust → Shared Pain → Admiration → Confidence → Belonging

This is **risk reversal through narrative.** By the time the ask arrives, the viewer already likes the founder, believes in the mission, and sees proof it works. The ask feels like an invitation, not a pitch.

## Rubric Scoring Analysis

### High-scoring dimensions

**Specificity (weight 1.5)** — Five concrete numbers in 80 words: "2019", "hundred farmers", "tripled", "six months", "7,000 members". Every claim is verifiable.

**Receptionist Test (weight 2.0)** — 5/5:
- What is it? Grass-fed meat delivery
- What's included? Box of meat each month
- How to start? Jump onto website
- What's the opportunity? Own shares
- Who's behind it? Dave, co-founder

**Objection Preemption (weight 1.5)** — Never says "this is safe" but the survival story (drought + bushfires → pivot → triple growth) answers the #1 objection ("is this business real?") through narrative, not assertion.

**CTA Clarity (weight 1.0)** — "Jump onto our website to learn more" — low-friction, funnel-matched for awareness stage. Asks for curiosity, not money.

### CFE compliance (meta-ad rules)

- No dollar amounts, no returns, no platform name
- "Own shares" and "learn more" — tease, don't detail
- Founder as guide, customer as hero
- Sequential commitment: "learn more" not "invest now"

## Why It Works: The Pivot Is the Proof

Most CFE pitches say "we're great, give us money." This script says "we nearly died, we adapted, it worked, now you can join." The wound→pivot→proof structure does three things simultaneously:

1. **Preempts the #1 objection** — is this business real? Yes, it survived 2019
2. **Creates emotional investment** before asking for financial investment
3. **Frames the ask as continuation** of a story already in motion, not a cold start

## Template for Reuse

```
[FOUNDER NAME + ROLE] (2s)
[WHAT THE COMPANY DOES — concrete, values-aligned] (4s)
[THE WOUND — specific challenge that forced change] (5s)
[THE PIVOT — what you did differently] (4s)
[PROOF 1 — supply/partner scale] (3s)
[PROOF 2 — growth metric + timeframe] (3s)
[PROOF 3 — customer/community number] (4s)
[THE ASK — identity-framed, not transactional] (4s)
[CTA — low-friction next step] (3s)
```

**Constraints:** ~80 words, ~33 seconds, minimum 3 concrete numbers, zero hard sell.
