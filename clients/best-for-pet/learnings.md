## What works (do more)

- **Story hooks with real moments.** Specific day, action, cost. "Last Tuesday, walked in, $0."
- **Quoted objection hooks.** Disbelief/scepticism frame earns permission to prove value.
- **If/then maths.** "If your pet sees the vet twice, you're already paying more." Let the maths sell.
- **Before/after cost contrast.** $150 vs $0. Show both sides.
- **Love/dread tension.** "Love your pet? Dread surprise bills?" Strongest empathy hook.
- **Confession hooks with genuine empathy.** Admitting embarrassment about skipping care is relatable.
- **Story-as-explanation.** Proving the product can be explained in one sentence IS the ad.
- **Insurance vs wellness comparison.** Side-by-side works when it solves real confusion.
- **Outcome & Results is the strongest angle.** Highest approval rate. Concrete proof resonates.

## What fails (never do)

- **Talking down to audience.** #1 kill signal. No "we'll wait", no commands ("stop"), no assumptions.
- **Em-dashes.** Remove all. Use full stops or commas. Em-dashes read cold/editorial.
- **Leading with price.** Lead with value/benefit. Price is the payoff, not the hook.
- **Self-deprecating the brand.** "Our website doesn't explain this well" kills trust.
- **Making the vet the villain.** The system is the problem, not the vet.
- **Unverified stats.** Every number must trace to the facts register.
- **Non-sequitur comparisons.** Netflix/gym analogies don't land for pet care.
- **Overclaiming.** "Every vet visit you'll ever need" is too broad. Be specific about what IS covered.
- **Asking people to do the work.** "Add it up" fails. Show them the answer.
- **Time-bound claims.** "Before Easter" breaks when shown later. Use visit-count framing.

## Copy rules

- **Under 500 characters** for body copy. If you can say it in 3 lines, don't use 8.
- **CTA: "Check availability at a clinic near you" / "Find a participating clinic."** NOT "see what's covered" / "your clinic."
- **"Find a participating clinic" not "your clinic."** Goal is to get them to switch.
- **Generic across formats.** Don't say "swipe" or "see the list" in primary text.
- **270+ clinics** (not 190+).
- **Frame toward goals, not away from pain.** "Sorted" > "subscription fatigue."
- **"Surprise bills" framing > "$0" framing.** Remove unpredictability, not just cost.
- **Predictability is the emotional payoff.** "Knowing what I pay each month" > "saving money."

## Consumer research insights (April 2026)

Key findings from consumer research (full doc: research/consumer-insights-pet-care.md):

- **Guilt is universal.** 74% felt guilty after delaying vet care. 100% of pet parents report guilt. Don't add guilt — resolve it.
- **Anxiety is about unpredictability.** 76-80% of AU owners worry about vet costs. The fear is "I don't know what this will cost." $50/month = certainty.
- **Insurance is trust-damaged.** Premiums doubling in 2-3 years, claims denied, pre-existing loopholes, 1.9/5 Medibank rating. "Not insurance" is emotional relief.
- **Pet parent identity is dominant.** 97% say pets are family, 82% say "like my child", 60% of Gen Z/Millennials prioritise pet spending over themselves.
- **Women 25-55 are primary decision-makers.** Higher humanisation, more guilt, more responsive to peer recommendations.
- **Health subscriptions are kept.** Highest retention tier. Under $50/month is the sweet spot. "Cancel anytime" addresses #1 subscription fear.
- **52% of owners skipped vet care last year (Gallup).** 71% cited cost. The audience already knows they should do more — they need the barrier removed.
- **The positive emotion of insurance is "saying yes to care."** BFP can own this emotional upside without the mechanics (claims, excess, fine print).

## Voice (see also tone-of-voice.md)

- Warm, alongside the reader, never above them
- Audience is ~75% female pet parents. Balance data with warmth.
- "Expensive" > "trap". No deception framing.
- No commands, no snark, no impatience
- Conversational Australian English
