# Best for Pet — Tone of Voice

This document is read before writing ANY ad copy, landing page, or client-facing content. It defines how Best for Pet should sound — not just what it says.

---

## The core feeling

Best for Pet is about **loving your pet without the financial stress**. Every piece of copy should make the reader feel:

1. **Warm** — like talking to a friend who also has pets
2. **Relieved** — the weight of vet costs lifting off their shoulders
3. **Good about themselves** — they're doing the right thing for their pet
4. **Understood** — someone gets what it's like to be a pet parent

The reader should never feel:
- Guilted into action
- Lectured about pet health
- Like they're reading a financial product comparison
- Stressed or anxious (even when we reference costs, the overall feeling should be positive)

---

## Voice characteristics

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Warm, conversational, like a friend | Corporate, clinical, like an insurance brochure |
| "Your pet" / "your dog" / "your cat" — personal | "Pets" / "animals" — generic |
| Positive framing: "cover everything" | Negative framing: "stop overpaying" |
| Light, approachable, occasionally playful | Heavy, serious, fear-based |
| Specific and real: "Luna's vaccinations are covered" | Abstract: "comprehensive preventative care solutions" |
| Short sentences. Easy rhythm. | Long compound sentences with multiple clauses |
| Australian English, relaxed | American English or formal British |

## The pet is the hero, not the price

Even in Price & Value ads, the emotional centre is the pet — not the dollar amount. The savings are what *enable* better care, not the point of the ad.

**Instead of:**
> $1,095 a year on routine care. Or $50/month. Do the maths.

**Try:**
> Imagine never hesitating to book Luna in when she's not herself. $50/month covers everything routine at your local vet — so you can just focus on her.

**Instead of:**
> Stop overpaying for vet care.

**Try:**
> More cuddles, fewer bills. $50/month covers vaccinations, check-ups, dental, and more.

## Emotional register by angle

Every angle should carry positive emotion, even when it references a pain point.

| Angle | Pain reference (brief) | Emotional resolution (dominant) |
|-------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Price & Value** | Vet bills add up | Freedom to care without counting dollars |
| **Simplicity & Clarity** | Confusing, too many costs | Relief — it's simple, it's sorted |
| **Safety & Risk** | Worry when something's wrong | Peace of mind — you can always go in |
| **Empathy & Understanding** | Guilt about skipping visits | You're a good pet parent, and this makes it easier |
| **Outcome & Results** | Used to spend too much | Joy of saving AND better care |

## Words and phrases to use

- "Your local vet" (not "participating clinics")
- "Covered" / "included" (not "entitled to" / "eligible for")
- "Simple" / "sorted" / "done" (not "comprehensive" / "solution")
- "Peace of mind" (sparingly — once per ad max)
- Pet names in examples: Luna, Max, Bella, Charlie, Milo
- "One monthly fee" (not "subscription" — avoids subscription fatigue)
- "No surprises" / "no bill shock"
- "Walk in and you're covered"

## Words and phrases to avoid

- "Subscription" / "subscribe" (use "plan" or "membership")
- "Premium" / "comprehensive" / "solution" (corporate jargon)
- "Act now" / "Don't miss out" (false urgency — this isn't a sale)
- "Worried?" / "Scared?" / "Can't afford?" (guilt/shame triggers)
- "Cheap" (undermines quality perception — use "affordable" or focus on value)
- "Insurance" without immediately clarifying "this is NOT insurance"
- "Terms and conditions apply" in body copy (save for disclaimers)

## The 3-second test

Read the ad aloud. In 3 seconds, does it make you feel:
- Warm? → Good
- Stressed? → Rewrite
- Nothing? → Add a pet, a name, or a real moment

## Example tone calibration

**Too cold (rational copywriter):**
> Best for Pet costs $50/month and includes unlimited vet consultations, vaccinations, $250 off dental, and annual health screening. No joining fee. Cancel anytime.

**Too warm (greeting card):**
> Your furry best friend deserves the world! Give them the gift of health with our amazing wellness plan!

**Just right (Best for Pet voice):**
> One monthly fee. Unlimited vet visits. All vaccinations. $250 off dental. Your local vet, your pet, sorted. $50/month — no joining fee, no lock-in, just better care for less.

## How this doc is used

1. **Before writing ads:** Read this first. Set the emotional register before choosing angles/hooks.
2. **During review:** Score ads against the 3-second test. If it feels cold or stressful, flag for tone revision.
3. **In the rubric:** Add "Emotional warmth" as dimension 11, or weight "Motivation Match" to include tonal warmth.
4. **In the skill:** The `/review-ads` skill should check tone against this doc when rewriting flagged ads.
