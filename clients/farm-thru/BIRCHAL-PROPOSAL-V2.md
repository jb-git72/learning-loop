# Pre-campaign offer v2: for review

**Date**: 2026-04-27
**Purpose**: Revised proposal in response to your 2026-04-27 feedback. Replaces the original Pre-campaign VIP Access pitch.

## 1. What changed

Two separate offers, instead of one bundled $5 VIP fee.

- **A. CSF investor waitlist**: free, email signup, priority SMS when the offer opens at Birchal
- **B. Save $10 product promo**: $5 for a $10 FarmThru voucher, redeem on products by 2026-04-30

Anyone can take A, B, both, or neither. The two offers are not linked.

## 2. The Save $10 promo (the new product)

- **Name**: Save $10 on FarmThru
- **Price**: $5 (Stripe charge, non-refundable once the voucher is issued)
- **Deliverable**: $10 voucher for FarmThru product orders of $25 or more, expires 2026-04-30
- **Sold at**: farmthru.com.au, public, visible to all visitors
- **Audience**: anyone who wants to try FarmThru products at a discount

## 3. The CSF investor waitlist (no change from current state)

- **Name**: FarmThru investor waitlist
- **Price**: free
- **Sold at**: join.farmthru.com.au
- **Deliverable**: priority SMS when the offer opens at Birchal, plus drip updates
- **Audience**: anyone interested in investing in FarmThru via the Birchal CSF offer

## 4. What the $5 explicitly does NOT buy

- Priority allocation
- Reserved shares
- Structural advantage at the Birchal facility
- Investor early access (already free with the waitlist)
- Extended payment terms on share applications (already provided free at the Birchal facility per Birchal's standard EOI)
- Any link to the share offer

The $5 buys product credit. The investor relationship is separate, free, and unchanged.

## 5. How we keep the two offers separate

- The voucher landing page lives on farmthru.com.au, not on the CSF waitlist site
- We run the voucher promo to lifestyle and food audiences, not investor audiences
- We track voucher-buyer overlap with the waitlist as an internal substance-monitoring KPI; we will pause the voucher promo and review the channel mix if non-waitlist buyers fall below ~25%. (We note ASIC's substance-over-form test under *Connective Services Pty Ltd v Slea* [2019] HCA 33 is "net balance of financial advantage", not a percentage threshold; the 25% figure is our internal proxy for keeping the channels operationally distinct.)
- Marketing copy never bundles the two offers
- The CSF waitlist thank-you page may mention the voucher only as one of several non-investor "ways to support FarmThru" of equivalent prominence, without stating or implying the voucher is investor-related; wording reviewed by Birchal before publication

## 6. Funds and refund handling

- Voucher purchases settle to FarmThru's operating account
- $5 is recognised as deferred revenue (voucher liability) on FarmThru's balance sheet, not held in trust. The CSF trust-account requirements in s981B + regs 7.8.01-7.8.03 (RG 262.103, RG 262.164-.165) bind the AFS-licensed intermediary that receives application money for share acquisition (Birchal). FarmThru's $5 is product consideration paid to the merchant for a $10 voucher; it is not application money and is not received by an AFS licensee for the purpose of acquiring shares
- No refund of the $5 once the voucher is issued
- If a voucher goes unredeemed by 2026-04-30 the $5 is retained per the promo terms (disclosed at purchase). The 3-year minimum expiry rule for gift cards in Schedule 2 of the Competition and Consumer Act 2010 (the Australian Consumer Law) does not apply because reg 89C of the Competition and Consumer Regulations 2010 exempts gift cards "supplied as part of a temporary marketing promotion to the purchaser of goods or services in connection with the purchase of the goods or services". The ACCC's published example of an exempt promotion is structurally identical (voucher tied to the purchase of a particular good within a defined campaign window)
- Voucher holders are unsecured creditors of FarmThru in insolvency (disclosed at purchase)

## 7. Mandatory disclosures on every CSF-touching channel

These appear on the CSF waitlist landing page, welcome email, and any drip email referencing the offer:

1. **s738ZG(6) safe-harbour statement**: *"Always consider the general CSF risk warning and offer document before investing."* (s738ZG(6) does not prescribe verbatim wording. RG 261.92 requires the statement to direct readers to the offer document and the general risk warning. We use the canonical formulation our internal compliance scorer enforces across all FarmThru CSF-touching content per `shared/regulatory/csf-australia/compliance_rules.json` rule ADV-001.)
2. **No-link statement**: *"The Save $10 product promo on farmthru.com.au is a separate consumer offer. Buying a voucher does not entitle you to invest, secure shares, or affect your access to any future investment offer."*

## 8. Mandatory disclosures on the Save $10 promo page

1. **Promo terms**: *"$5 buys a $10 voucher for FarmThru product orders of $25 or more. Expires 2026-04-30. Not redeemable for cash. No refunds once issued. This is a temporary marketing promotion under reg 89C of the Competition and Consumer Regulations 2010; the 3-year minimum expiry rule for gift cards under the Australian Consumer Law does not apply."*
2. **No-investment-link statement**: *"This voucher is a product offer. It does not entitle you to invest in FarmThru, secure shares, or affect any future investment offer."*

## 9. Phrases we will never use

- "VIP investor"
- "Pay $5 for early access to invest"
- "Voucher buyers get priority"
- "Investment perk"
- Any phrase linking the voucher to investor status or share access
