# FarmThru — Common Learnings (loaded for ALL content types)

See also: learnings-meta-ad.md, learnings-landing-page.md, learnings-email.md

## CRITICAL RULES

- NEVER use em dashes or en dashes. Use full stops, commas, or colons.
- Sentences: 13-18 words. Short paragraphs with line breaks.
- NEVER use doubt language ("we can't promise..."). Reframe positively.
- NEVER lead with "regenerative". Too niche. Supporting detail only.
- NEVER say "days old". Say "straight from farm to hub."
- Distance: use multipliers ("10x less"), not raw km.
- No fabricated stats. No named competitors. No Title Case.
- No "delivered to your door" — hub-and-collect model.
- No "same beef"/"same quality" — the point is it's DIFFERENT.

## What Works (all content types)

- "Own a piece of the grocery store that pays farmers first" (BR-110 standout)
- "Values and money finally pointing the same direction" (CFE concept)
- Dual CTA: waitlist is for investors AND customers. Always mention both.
- Phrases: "own a piece of it", "be a partner", "pays farmers first"
- "Straight from farm to hub" beats "days old"
- "Same cut, completely different impact on farmers"
- Comparison: 2,400km supermarket vs direct. Use multipliers.
- Rachel's voice: authentic, but transition to FarmThru naturally
- Customer pain -> acknowledgment -> proof -> transition -> CTA

## Content Type Rules

- **Meta ads**: EITHER brand/product OR CFE. No investment details. No VIP/$5.
- **Landing pages**: Investment details, VIP deposit, compliance HERE. Subhead max 120 chars.
- **Emails**: VIP upsell, investment terms HERE. Single CTA. Founder voice.
