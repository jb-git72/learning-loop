# Email Learnings

## Frameworks
- **PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)**: Best for pain-driven emails. Name a pain, twist the knife, present relief.
- **BAB (Before-After-Bridge)**: Best for aspiration emails. Current reality, desired state, how to get there.
- **Rule of One**: One big idea, one reader, one offer, one CTA. +371% clicks for single-CTA emails.

## Subject Lines
- Under 50 characters / 7 words (+12% opens, +75% CTR)
- Formulas: question, curiosity gap, number, announcement, scarcity
- Personalized with {first_name}: +26% opens

## Structure
- Short paragraphs (max 3 sentences each)
- Single CTA per email
- Founder voice (Rachel & the FarmThru team)
- Sensory anchoring: min 2 sensory details (crisp, earthy, tender, not "fresh" or "nice")
- Named-farm story: farm, farmer, product, day. Connect to reader's kitchen in 3 sentences.

## Email Drip Sequence (4 phases)
1. Pre-launch warmup (1/week, 4-6 weeks): relationship + education
2. Launch week (3-5 in 7 days): day-1 funding must hit 25%+ of goal
3. Mid-campaign (1-2/week): momentum + social proof
4. Final 48 hours (4 emails: 48h/24h/6h/1h): FOMO close, 20-30% of total funding

## VIP vs Non-VIP
- VIP deposits convert 20-40% vs 1-5% email-only
- Non-VIP emails always include upgrade CTA
- VIP emails reward commitment with exclusive access language

## Investment Disclaimer
If the email mentions investment, Birchal, equity, crowdfunding, $50, $5 deposit, or waitlist: add as the FINAL paragraph before sign-off: "This is not financial advice. Consider seeking independent financial advice. Full details in the disclosure document on Birchal."
