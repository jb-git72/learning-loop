# Meta-Ad Learnings

## What Works
- **BR-110 standout**: "Own a piece of the grocery store that pays farmers first." More "putting farmers first" messaging.
- **"Values and money finally pointing the same direction"** — big concept for CFE. Explore multiple forms.
- **BR-107 structure**: comparison hook (if-then), supply chain contrast, narrative arc
- **Dual CTA: invest + customer**: Waitlist is for investors AND customers. Always mention both.
- **"Straight from farm to hub"** beats "days old". Reframe freshness as directness.
- **Same cut, different impact**: "Same cut of meat, completely different impact on farmers."
- **Comparison data**: 2,400km supermarket vs direct. Use multipliers ("10x less distance"), not raw km.
- Sentences: 13-18 words. Killed ads averaged 23-28.
- Customer-benefit stats only, never market stats.

## Structure
- Customer pain/question -> acknowledgment -> product proof (specific) -> transition ("why act now") -> CTA
- Meta ads: EITHER brand/product OR CFE — never both. No investment details.
- Description field: complement headline, don't repeat it.
- Founder story as PROOF LAYER — Rachel validates, doesn't lead.

## Anti-Patterns
- No "$50", "$10K", "equity crowdfunding", "Birchal", "Not financial advice"
- No VIP/$5 deposit — landing pages/emails only
- No market stats ($130B, 24% YoY)
- No "startup" — too cold for a food brand
- No bolted-on investment terms — tease with "be part of it", never detail
