# Meta-Ad Learnings

## What Works
- "Own a piece of the grocery store that pays farmers first."
- "Values and money finally pointing the same direction" (CFE big idea)
- Comparison hook (if-then) + supply chain contrast
- Waitlist serves investors AND customers — mention both
- "Straight from farm to hub" beats "days old"
- Multipliers ("10x less distance") not raw km
- Sentences 13-18 words. Customer-benefit stats only.

## Structure
- Pain/question → acknowledgment → specific proof → why-now → CTA
- EITHER brand/product OR CFE, never both. No investment details.
- Description complements headline. Founder story is PROOF LAYER, not lead.

## Anti-Patterns
- No "$50", "$10K", "equity crowdfunding", "Birchal", "Not financial advice"
- No VIP/$5 deposit (LP/email only), no "startup"
- No bolted-on investment — tease with "be part of it", never detail

## Values-Aligned Angles (Empathy + Social Belonging)
Tap shared values, not features. Lead with felt gap or identity.

**Empathy** — name the gap reader already feels. Two-clause headline, period mid:
- "This food exists. It's just never been easy to get to."
- "There was no good way to buy farm-quality groceries in Sydney."

**Social Belonging** — identity through what you eat / who you support:
- "FarmThru families know who grew their food."
- "Know who grew your food." / "The food does the talking."

## High-Performance Levers ($2-lead ad)
- **Curiosity-gap hook**: "About to open something up that's never been done with a grocery store in Australia." Narrow novelty, category+geo bounded.
- **Ownership > investment**: "own a piece of", "be part of" beats "invest from $X".
- **Soft scarcity**: "opens soon", "first in gets first access" beats "act now".
- **Outcome-stated CTA**: "Leave your email — we'll tell you when it goes live."
- **Founder voice**: "we've built", "we're about to" — past-tense build language.
- **CSF as asterisked footnote** in final paragraph, never in body.
