### Counter offset (post-canary): why it's harmless

After cleanup, counter sits at **128** with `db_public = 127`. The next genuine public signup will land at **position 129**, leaving position 128 permanently unoccupied. This is:

- **Intentional**: `add_signup` uses `Increment(1)` on the counter (race-safe atomic write) and counters never decrement after delete (otherwise concurrent writes could collide on the same position).
- **User-visible only on the new signup's thank-you page**: it would say "Your queue position: #129" + "129 people on the waitlist". Internally consistent.
- **LP `signupCount` remains correct**: it's computed independently from `len(get_signups(exclude_test=True))`, NOT from the counter, so the LP shows 128 once a real signup lands.
- **Self-corrects on next renumber**: any future renumber pass collapses the gap to contiguous 1..N.

**Conclusion**: a 1-position offset is the cost of a real end-to-end canary test against a counter-based system. Acceptable. Not a regression of PR #116.
