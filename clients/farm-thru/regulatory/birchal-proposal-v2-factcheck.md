# Proposal v2 fact-check (RG 261/262 + Pt 6D.3A + ACL)

**Date**: 2026-04-27
**Subject**: `clients/farm-thru/BIRCHAL-PROPOSAL-V2.md` (PR #106, on main)
**Verdict**: PASS-WITH-REVISIONS

## Executive verdict

- **Structure is sound.** Decoupling — free CSF waitlist + standalone $5-for-$10 product voucher sold publicly on `farmthru.com.au`, redemption before the offer opens — is the cleanest paid-product architecture short of dropping the charge entirely. Substance-over-form risk under s738ZE is materially reduced versus the original VIP fee, but the substance test must be policed in copy and operations, not just the proposal text.
- **Eight wording-level fixes required before sending.** All are wording-level; none requires structural redesign. Top four: (1) drop the "(verbatim)" claim on the s738ZG(6) statement and align with the engine's canonical line; (2) reframe the "25% non-waitlist" benchmark as an internal monitoring proxy, not a regulatory threshold (Connective Services v Slea [2019] HCA 33's "net balance of financial advantage" is the actual substance test); (3) cite reg 89C of the Competition and Consumer Regulations 2010 expressly as the ACL gift-card carve-out; (4) add "extended payment terms" to the not-buying list since Birchal already provides them free.
- **Birchal help-doc claim re-confirmed live (2026-04-27)**: "You may also get updates, early access to the CSF offer and be eligible for delayed payment terms and early bird rewards." (https://help.birchal.com/en/articles/5018296). The "early access (already free with the waitlist)" claim in section 4 is sound; "delayed payment terms" should be added explicitly.
- **Strongest residual risk** is RG 262.144 — Birchal's gatekeeper duty to refuse to publish if it has "reason to believe" pre-offer misleading conduct has occurred. Section 5 commitments are the right operational hedges; they need to be carried into the touchpoint copy and the CSF-thank-you page caveat needs tightening.
- **Lawyer-verify list**: four narrow items, all of which the prior solutions agent also flagged. None blocks the verdict.

## Section-by-section findings

### Section 1 — What changed

**PASS.** Two-offer split is substance-defensible. Up-front "the two offers are not linked" framing pre-empts the substance lens (RG 261.96, s1041H).

### Section 2 — Save $10 promo

**PASS.** Standalone consumer product, public, redeemable on a real shop by 2026-04-30. This is the structure Allens analysis (https://www.allens.com.au/insights-news/insights/2019/11/new-laws-on-gift-cards-what-you-need-to-know-now/) identifies as carve-out-eligible under reg 89C of the Competition and Consumer Regulations 2010.

### Section 3 — CSF investor waitlist

**PASS.** Free, separate domain (`join.farmthru.com.au`), unchanged. Eliminates s738ZE / s260A / RG 261.59 / RG 262.151 surface area on the investor side — no money flows to FMTH "in connection with" the CSF offer per RG 261.90.

### Section 4 — What the $5 explicitly does NOT buy

**PASS-WITH-REVISIONS.** "Investor early access (already free with the waitlist)" verified against Birchal's help article (re-fetched 2026-04-27). **Missing**: extended payment terms — Birchal's same help article confirms they provide "delayed payment terms" free; this should appear in the not-buying list to pre-empt Birchal's observation. See Required revision #1.

### Section 5 — Decoupling commitments

**PASS-WITH-REVISIONS.** Five bullets correctly address operational separation. Two issues:

**The 25% benchmark is not a regulatory test.** ASIC has no percentage threshold for substance-over-form on financial assistance. The High Court's formulation in *Connective Services Pty Ltd v Slea Pty Ltd* [2019] HCA 33 is "net balance of financial advantage" — i.e., does the conduct ease the financial burden of, or improve the person's net balance of financial advantage in connection with, the share acquisition. The 25% figure is a sensible internal commercial KPI (if 75%+ of voucher buyers are also waitlist signups, the channels are de facto bundled regardless of any proposal text), but should be reframed so Birchal does not read it as FMTH proposing a regulatory test it then audits FMTH against. See Required revision #2.

**The CSF-thank-you-page caveat is a substance-leakage channel.** RG 261.90 expressly extends the prohibition to conduct "before or after the investor acquires shares" — a waitlist signup directed from CSF channel to product channel within the same session is in scope. Two safer wordings: (a) strict (no mention at all on any CSF-touching page), or (b) caveated with Birchal-pre-publication-review and equal-prominence framing. Current draft is closer to (b); recommend tightening. See Required revision #3.

### Section 6 — Funds and refund handling

**PASS-WITH-REVISIONS.** The substance test for whether the $5 is "application money" is correct: ASIC looks at substance not label (RG 262.151, RG 261.59, Connective Services). Five conditions let an issuer treat a $5 charge as ordinary revenue: (a) real consumer deliverable worth more than the price (here: $10 voucher), (b) severable in time and subject matter from the share offer (redeem by 2026-04-30, groceries not investment access), (c) sold to non-prospective-investor buyers too (section 5 substance commitments), (d) no marketing implication that $5 = price-of-investing-earlier (section 9), (e) accounted for as deferred revenue not trust money. All five are addressed.

But: "is product consideration not application money" is a legal characterisation that depends on those five conditions actually holding in execution — it is not self-executing. The proposal would be stronger if it cited s981B + regs 7.8.01–7.8.03 (and RG 262.103, RG 262.164–.165) as the rules that **do not apply** because FMTH is not an AFS licensee receiving application money — Birchal is. See Required revision #4.

**Voucher expiry retention.** The 3-year minimum gift-card expiry rule in ACL Schedule 2 of the Competition and Consumer Act 2010 (in force from 1 Nov 2019, Treasury Laws Amendment (Gift Cards) Act 2018) does not apply because reg 89C of the Competition and Consumer Regulations 2010 exempts gift cards "supplied as part of a temporary marketing promotion to the purchaser of goods or services in connection with the purchase of the goods or services". The ACCC's published example of an exempt promotion is structurally identical (a voucher tied to the purchase of a particular good within a defined campaign window). Allens treats this carve-out as broad and additionally identifies "discount vouchers on market value items" as a separate exempt category (which the FMTH voucher arguably is — $10 against $25+ orders is a discount mechanic). Cite reg 89C explicitly so Birchal's compliance team does not have to derive it. See Required revision #5.

**Insolvency disclosure** ("voucher holders are unsecured creditors") is materially correct.

### Section 7 — CSF-channel disclosures

**PASS-WITH-REVISIONS.** Two issues:

**The "(verbatim)" label on s738ZG(6) is wrong-by-construction.** s738ZG(6) does **not** prescribe verbatim wording for the safe-harbour statement. RG 261.92: "the advertisement or publication includes a statement that investors should consider the offer document and the general risk warning in deciding whether to apply under the offer." Substance is what is required; any wording conveying that meaning satisfies the safe harbour. Reg 6D.3A.03 (offer-document Section 1) and reg 6D.3A.10 (intermediary platform) prescribe the **risk warning text itself** verbatim — they do **not** prescribe the s738ZG(6) advertising-statement text.

**The proposal's wording vs the engine's canonical line.** Proposal: "*Always consider the CSF offer document and the general risk warning.*" Engine (`shared/regulatory/csf-australia/compliance_rules.json` rule ADV-001): "*Always consider the general CSF risk warning and offer document before investing.*" Both convey the s738ZG(6) meaning; both are safe. But the engine's line (a) makes the call-to-action explicit ("before investing"), (b) is what every other piece of FMTH content uses, and (c) avoids creating a regression risk in the scoring engine when touchpoint copy is checked. Use the engine's line everywhere and drop "(verbatim)". See Required revision #6.

**No-link statement structural function (7.2).** Wording is materially correct, but a disclosure-only no-link statement is not, on its own, structural separation — substance-over-form (Connective Services, RG 261.90) looks at conduct. The disclosure documents the operational separation in section 5; it is not the separation itself. This is the correct division of labour, but the proposal could state it more clearly to head off the predictable Birchal critique.

**Required-but-missing on CSF-touching channels.** Forward-looking-statement caveats (RG 261.197, RG 261.198, s738U(2); engine ADV-004) and anti-hawking compliance (s736; engine ADV-003) are touchpoint-copy gates, not section-7 additions. Mention in section 11 ask. See Required revision #8.

### Section 8 — Promo-page disclosures

**PASS-WITH-REVISIONS.** The promo page does **not** need an s738ZG(6) statement, because it is not advertising "a CSF offer or intended CSF offer" (RG 261.94–.95: capture is for statements that "directly or indirectly refer to the offer or intended offer" or are "reasonably likely to induce people to apply for shares"). The promo page, as designed, does neither. **This is the structural payoff of the decoupling.** Section 8.1 and 8.2 wording is materially correct; recommend adding the reg 89C citation to 8.1 so the disclosure doubles as the legal basis. See Required revision #7.

### Section 9 — Phrases never to use

**PASS.** Five-bullet list captures the substance-collapse triggers correctly. "VIP investor", "Pay $5 for early access to invest", "Voucher buyers get priority", "Investment perk", and "Any phrase linking the voucher to investor status or share access" are exactly the formulations that would re-import s738ZE / s738ZA / RG 261.96 risk. Already-engine-enforced phrasings ("reserve your spot", "$5 deposit") do not require a section 9 edit — covered by `compliance_rules.json` ADV-002 and `clients/farm-thru/rules.json` FMTH-PRIORITY-001.

### Section 10 — Touchpoints

**PASS.** Five touchpoints listed. Recommend mentioning in section 11 that all touchpoint copy will be run through the engine's compliance scorer before submission to Birchal — gives Birchal an audit trail. See Required revision #8.

### Section 11 — Ask

**PASS-WITH-REVISIONS.** Three asks listed. Two further asks would close the loop: pre-publication review of the CSF-waitlist thank-you page wording (per section 5 caveat); confirmation Birchal's RG 262.144 gatekeeper review treats this design as "not misleading". See Required revision #8.

### Revision 4 — Section 6: cite the regulatory basis for deferred-revenue treatment

**Current** (section 6, second bullet):
> - $5 is recognised as deferred revenue, not held in trust, because it is product consideration not application money

**Replace with**:

FarmThru's $5 is product consideration paid to the merchant for a $10 voucher; it is not application money and is not received by an AFS licensee for the purpose of acquiring shares.

**Citation**: RG 262.164; RG 262.103; s981B; regs 7.8.01–7.8.03.

## Lawyer-verify list

Items the agent could not resolve from public sources, that must be confirmed by a CSF-experienced lawyer before sending to Birchal:

1. **Reg 89C "temporary marketing promotion" carve-out applies to a $5-for-$10 voucher tied to a 3-day-to-3-week pre-CSF-launch campaign.** Allens analysis treats the carve-out as broad; ACCC's published example (whitegood-tied $50 voucher) is structurally similar; the binary risk on the proposal hangs on the carve-out applying. A written one-line confirmation from a CSF-or-ACL-experienced lawyer is the cheapest insurance available.

2. **s738ZE characterisation of the new structure.** The original VIP fee was clearly capturable. The new structure (standalone $5-for-$10 voucher, redemption before offer opens, separate channel, public to non-investors) materially reduces s738ZE surface area, but ASIC's substance-over-form posture (Connective Services) means the question is fact-specific. A lawyer should opine on (a) does the structure as designed avoid s738ZE capture, and (b) what operational facts would reactivate capture if they drift in execution.

3. **The CSF-waitlist thank-you-page mention of the voucher.** This is the one substance-leakage channel that survives the decoupling. A lawyer should opine on whether ANY mention of the voucher on a CSF-touching page is safe (caveated version), or whether the strict version (no mention at all) is required.

4. **The standalone-marketing test.** Birchal will likely ask "if FMTH had no CSF round opening soon, would FMTH still run this voucher promo at all?" The proposal needs to be able to answer "yes" with operational evidence (a marketing plan that does not reference the CSF offer, a target audience of food/lifestyle buyers, a media buy that does not overlap with investor audiences). A lawyer should confirm what operational evidence is sufficient.

These four items are the same bucket the prior solutions agent flagged. None has been resolved by public-source research.

## Verdict justification

**Verdict: PASS-WITH-REVISIONS.**

The proposal is structurally sound. The decoupling — free CSF waitlist + standalone $5-for-$10 product voucher sold publicly on `farmthru.com.au` with redemption before the offer opens — is the cleanest paid-product architecture available short of dropping the charge entirely. It directly addresses the four Birchal concerns: (1) what's genuinely additional — a $10 voucher worth more than the $5 paid, plus an explicit not-buying list; (2) refund + financial assistance — voucher redeemed for product before the offer opens, on a separate channel and audience, materially reducing s738ZE / s260A surface area; (3) where the funds are held — deferred revenue / voucher liability on FMTH's balance sheet, never application money, never received by an AFS licensee for share acquisition; (4) risk to investors — honest insolvency disclosure, voucher concretely valued at 2x the price paid, the investor relationship (free waitlist) carries no money at risk.

The eight wording-level revisions tighten the disclosure stack without changing the structure. None requires structural redesign; all can be applied in a single commit. The remaining four lawyer-verify items are the same items the solutions agent flagged; none changes the verdict; all four should be in writing before launch.

**Caveat**: this fact-check is a research summary, not legal advice. Local copies of RG 261 and RG 262 (`shared/regulatory/csf-australia/`) are the operative sources cited; the wording of RG 261.92, RG 261.88–.90 (s738ZE), RG 262.103/.144/.151/.164/.165, the *Connective Services* "net balance of financial advantage" formulation, the reg 89C carve-out, the ACCC published example, and the Birchal EOI public guidance are confirmed against primary sources and the local engine rules. The s738ZE characterisation of the new (post-decoupling) structure should be confirmed by a CSF-experienced lawyer before sending to Birchal.
