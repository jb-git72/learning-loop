We wanted to know where grocery store beef actually comes from. So we checked. Nine different cuts from three major chains. Not one label named a farm. Not one told you how far it travelled. Industry data suggests that journey averages 2,400 kilometres. FarmThru is a different kind of grocery store. Every product traces to a named regenerative farm. You will know exactly what you are eating.

What 2,400 kilometres does to your food

Supermarket beef can spend 14 to 21 days in cold storage before it reaches the shelf. That is not fresh. That is logistics.

FarmThru sources straight from farm to hub to your kitchen. 100% grass-fed. Pasture-raised. No hormones, no antibiotics, no feedlot finishing.

You collect from your nearest hub. Brookvale is open Monday to Friday. The difference is not subtle. You will taste it in the first bite.

You will know every farmer by name

Rachel Ward runs a regenerative farm in Kempsey, NSW. She is a key partner and supplier to FarmThru.

Bundarra Farm provides pork belly and pork and fennel salami. Collins Wild Caught brings wild-caught Alaskan salmon from the South Coast of NSW. Farmer Brown supplies eggs. Paris Creek Farms in Meadows, SA delivers carbon-neutral biodynamic dairy.

Six farms. Real names. Real places. That is what 100% sourced from regenerative farms actually looks like.

How it works: 3 steps to better food

1. Browse the shop online. Regenerative beef, pasture-raised chicken, wild-caught salmon, heritage pork, eggs, and dairy.

2. Place your order. No subscription. No commitment. Order when you want.

3. Collect from your nearest hub. Brookvale hub is open Monday to Friday. Straight from farm to hub, ready for your kitchen.
