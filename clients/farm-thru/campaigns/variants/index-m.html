For years I told myself supermarket food was fine. Fresh enough. Good enough. Then I flipped over a pack of beef mince and saw no farm name. No region. Just a barcode and a use-by date.

I started asking questions. The answers were worse than I expected. Produce travelling over 2,400km before reaching the shelf. Farmers earning cents while retail prices climbed.

So we built something different. A grocery store where every product traces back to a named farm. Order online, collect from Brookvale. No middlemen inflating the price.

The problem nobody talks about at the checkout

Your supermarket trolley looks full. But the supply chain behind it is broken.

Produce travels up to 10x further than it needs to. Farmers sell at a loss so retailers can run specials. And you pay premium prices for food with no name, no story, no traceability.

This isn't a niche concern. It's the weekly shop for every household in Australia. The system rewards distance and anonymity. It punishes the people who actually grow your food.

How FarmThru fixes it in three steps

Step 1: We partner directly with farms across NSW. Around 15 to 20 named producers, from regenerative beef to seasonal greens.

Step 2: You order online whenever you want. No subscription. No lock-in. No membership fees. Buy in bulk and save more.

Step 3: Collect from our Brookvale hub, Monday to Friday. That's it. Farm to hub to you. No unnecessary middlemen adding cost.

You can see every farm behind every product

Brookvale is our first hub: Unit 23, 10-18 Orchard Rd. We also offer delivery across Sydney, Central Coast, and Wollongong.

Every product lists the farm it came from. Not a region. Not a state. The actual farm.

This is what "we'll show you where your food comes from" looks like. Not a marketing slogan. A traceable supply chain you can verify yourself.
