Every week, Australian farmers sell food below cost so supermarkets can run a promotion. You already know this. You've seen the headlines, felt the frustration, maybe even changed how you shop.

But shopping differently doesn't fix the model. Owning a different model might.

FarmThru is a farm-direct grocery store. No unnecessary middlemen. Customers order online and collect from our Brookvale hub. We're opening equity crowdfunding on Birchal so you can become a co-owner, not just a customer.

You've seen this problem up close

You flip over a pack of mince. No farm name. No region. Just a barcode and a use-by date.

You buy "free range" eggs and wonder what that actually means. You pay more for organic, hoping the farmer sees the difference. They rarely do.

The average supermarket supply chain moves food roughly 2,400km before it reaches a shelf. Every kilometre adds cost. Every middleman takes margin. The farmer absorbs the loss.

This isn't a broken system. It's working exactly as designed. Just not for you, and not for the people growing your food.

What makes FarmThru a different investment thesis

FarmThru cuts the chain between farm and family. We partner with 15 to 20 farms and suppliers across NSW. Food goes straight from farm to hub. Customers order online, collect when it suits them. No lock-in. No subscription. No membership fees.

The model is called hub-and-collect. It keeps overhead low and margins honest. Farmers get paid fairly because we're not extracting margin at every step.

Direct-to-consumer food is growing at 24% year on year in Australia. The $130B grocery market is shifting. FarmThru is positioned where that shift is heading: transparent sourcing, bulk buying savings, and a supply chain you can actually trace.
