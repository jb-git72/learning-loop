You spend hundreds on groceries every month. You assume the farmer gets a fair cut. They don't.

Supermarket supply chains pass your dollar through warehouses, wholesalers, and distribution centres. By the time it reaches the farm, there's almost nothing left.

FarmThru cuts all of that out. Farm to hub to you. Zero warehouses. Zero wholesalers. Your money goes where it should: to the people feeding you.

Now you can own a piece of the grocery store that pays farmers first.

You're already paying. The question is who gets it.

Every week, Australian families hand over grocery money trusting that someone, somewhere, is being paid fairly.

But the standard supply chain travels roughly 2,400km before food hits a shelf. Each stop takes a cut. The farmer absorbs the loss.

This isn't a broken system. It's working exactly as designed. It's just not designed for farmers. Or for you.

FarmThru exists because we believe your grocery dollar should do two things: feed your family well and pay the people who grew that food properly.

How it works: three steps, no middlemen

1. Browse and order online from 15 to 20 partner farms and suppliers across NSW and beyond.

2. We coordinate directly with farms like Bundarra Farm, Collins Wild Caught on the South Coast, Paris Creek Farms in South Australia, and Farmer Brown for eggs.

3. Collect from our Brookvale hub, Monday to Friday. Or choose delivery across Sydney, Central Coast, and Wollongong.

No subscription. No lock-in. No membership fees. Order when you want. Buy in bulk and save more.

Every product traces back to a named farm. We'll show you where your food comes from.

Real farms. Real names. That's the whole point.

Pork belly from Bundarra Farm. Wild-caught Alaskan salmon from Collins Wild Caught on the NSW South Coast. Carbon-neutral biodynamic dairy from Paris Creek Farms in Meadows, SA. Eggs from Farmer Brown.

Rachel Ward, actor turned regenerative farming advocate, runs Rachel's Farm in Kempsey, NSW. She's a key partner and supplier to FarmThru.

When Rachel talks about regenerative farming, she's standing in the paddock where it happens. That's the standard we hold every supplier to.

We only stock food we'd feed our own families. If we can't name the farm, it doesn't go on the shelf.
