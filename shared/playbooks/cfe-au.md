# CFE-AU Pre-Campaign Creative Playbook

Source: ASIC RG 261/262 + Farm Thru live $2-lead ad (LIVE-FMTH-NEVER-DONE) + PR #125 hypothesis validation 2026-04-28 + Motion Creative Benchmarks 2026 ($1.3B Meta spend).

Phase: pre-campaign (before offer document live).

---

## What This Phase Is For

Waitlist capture only. Converting attention to email opt-ins, not investors. Investment mechanics (dollar amounts, equity %, return projections) are prohibited in ads per ASIC RG 261.96/99. Every ad must include the general CSF risk warning per s738ZG(6) (compliance rule ADV-001).

---

## Hook Priority

1. **curiosity_gap_narrow_bound** (weight 3.0) — The gold standard for this phase. PR #125 H1 CONFIRMED: removing the geographic/category bound dropped composite 0.79 to 0.61 (-0.19). Pattern: "never been done with [category] in [geography]." Lead with the bound, not the proof.

2. **ownership_invitation** (weight 2.5) — PR #125 H2 CONFIRMED: removing "own a piece of" framing caused an identical magnitude drop (-0.19). Every pre-campaign ad should contain at least one ownership phrase in the first two paragraphs.

3. **exclusivity** (weight 1.8) — "First in gets first access", "registration opens soon." Future soft scarcity, not deadline pressure.

4. **founder_voice_build** (weight 1.5) — "We've built", "we're about to." Authority layer in body, NOT as opening line. PR #125 H3 DISCONFIRMED founder voice as hook: curiosity gap outperforms by ~0.06 composite.

**Do NOT use as opening hook:** price_anchor (ADV-002 blocks specific $), urgency with hard deadlines (misleading before offer), founder voice alone (H3 disconfirmed).

---

## Audience Motivation Hierarchy

In order: **ownership > returns > novelty > belonging.**

- Ownership is the primary driver. "Own a piece of" activates identity, not transaction. This is the lever that converts curiosity to action.
- Returns cannot be stated in pre-campaign (ADV-002). Imply through outcome language: "pays farmers first", "community-owned model."
- Novelty lands when narrowed: category + geography + mechanic. Generic "disruption" language is ignored.
- Belonging works as a proof layer in the body, not as a hook.

---

## Scarcity Register

Allowed (soft/future): "opens soon", "first in gets first access", "we'll tell you the moment it goes live", "registration opens soon."

Prohibited (hard): "act now", "last chance", "limited time only", "closes in N hours", "only N spots left at this price."

Hard scarcity before an offer document exists risks misleading conduct (ACL s18). ASIC also views manufactured urgency as a trust signal problem in CSF ads.

---

## CTA Conventions

CTA alone scores lower than CTA + outcome statement. The outcome statement anchors the reader's expectation and reduces friction.

Pre-campaign CTAs: Join the Waitlist / Get Early Access / Leave Your Email.

Required outcome statement in body: "Leave your email -- we'll tell you the moment it goes live."

This pattern is taken directly from LIVE-FMTH-NEVER-DONE, which delivered $2 CPLs.

---

## CSF Compliance Placement

Asterisked footnote in the final paragraph only. Never mid-body.

Canonical: "Always consider the general CSF risk warning and offer document before investing."

Compliance language in the body dampens momentum. Final-paragraph placement satisfies ADV-001 while preserving copy flow. Live ad placed it as a starred line after the CTA -- scores 5/5 on the csf_placement rubric dimension.

## Gold-Standard Ad Structure (LIVE-FMTH-NEVER-DONE)

Opening: curiosity_gap_narrow_bound + category + geography.
Para 2: Ownership invitation + specifics (farms, hub, scale).
Para 3: Soft scarcity + outcome-stated CTA.
Footnote: CSF risk warning, asterisked.

Headline: Ownership frame, sentence case.

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Specific investment dollar amounts -- ADV-002 BLOCKING.
- Birchal, equity %, return projections -- prohibited pre-campaign.
- Hard scarcity before offer exists -- misleading under ACL.
- Founder voice as opening line -- H3 disconfirmed.
- Investment language ("invest from $X") -- reframe as ownership.
- Title Case headlines.
- Em dashes or en dashes.
- Generic novelty ("revolutionary", "disruptive") without geo/category bound.
