# Fashion & Apparel — Industry Creative Playbook

## What Works
- Newness hooks: "just dropped", "first look", "introducing" — highest hit rate in fashion
- Unconventional text placement and post-it style formats
- Quiz/poll-style engagement ("Which style are you?")
- Meme-adjacent creative that feels native to social feeds
- FOMO through real scarcity: specific stock counts, waitlist numbers
- Exclusivity: early access, limited runs, member-only drops
- Pattern interrupts that don't look like ads

## What Fails
- Polished lookbook imagery without a hook or reason to act
- Generic "shop now" without specificity (what, why, why now)
- Over-styled creative that reads as "ad" immediately
- Price-first approaches for premium/luxury positioning
- Long-form copy without visual novelty — fashion is visual-first
- Urgency without real deadlines ("limited time" with no end date)

## Best Combinations
- exclusivity-status + newness + aida (product launches)
- quality-craft + curiosity + curiosity-gap (heritage brands)
- transformation-storytelling + fomo + social-proof-stack (DTC brands)
